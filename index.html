pandas as pd Lets you work with data in tables (DataFrames) matplotlib Lets you make charts and graphs seaborn Makes prettier, easier-to-read statistical plot

2.loading dataset

df.head()
df.tail()

3.Checking Dataset Info

print(df.info())
Displays column names, data types, and checks for missing values

4.Total units of smartphone sold

#Total units sold per brand
brand_sales = df.groupby('brand')['units_sold'].sum().sort_values(ascending=False)
print(brand_sales)
sns.barplot(x=brand_sales.values, y=brand_sales.index)
plt.title('Total Units Sold per Brand')
plt.xlabel('Units Sold')
plt.show()

5.Average price per brand

avg_price = df.groupby('brand')['price_usd'].mean().sort_values(ascending=False)
print(avg_price)
sns.barplot(x=avg_price.values, y=avg_price.index)
plt.title('Average Price per Brand')
plt.xlabel('Price (USD)')
plt.show()
This code calculates the average price (in USD) for each brand and sorts them from highest to lowest. It then plots a horizontal bar chart to display the average price per brand.

6.Boxplot: Rating by Brand

sns.boxplot(data=df, x='brand', y='rating')
plt.title('Rating Distribution by Brand')
plt.xlabel('Brand')
plt.ylabel('Rating')
plt.xticks(rotation=45)
plt.show()
Ouput:
This code creates a boxplot to show how ratings are distributed for each brand, helping compare variation and median ratings across brands.

7.Top 5 most expensive smartphones

top_expensive = df.nlargest(5, 'price_usd')[['brand', 'model', 'price_usd']]

Ouput:
Show top 5 expensive smartphones

8.Cheapest smartphone per brand

cheapest_per_brand = df.loc[df.groupby('brand')['price_usd'].idxmin()][['brand', 'model', 'price_usd']]
print(cheapest_per_brand)
Ouput:
Shows cheapest smartphone per brand

9.Market share pie chart by brand

brand_share = df.groupby('brand')['units_sold'].sum()
brand_share.plot(kind='pie', autopct='%1.1f%%', startangle=90)
plt.title('Market Share by Brand')
plt.ylabel('')
plt.show()
This code calculates each brandâ€™s total units sold and shows their share as a pie chart, with percentages labeled to compare market share visually.
